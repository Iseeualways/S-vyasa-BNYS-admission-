BNYS Admission Process

01
Check Your Eligibility

Before applying, candidates must ensure that they meet the prescribed eligibility criteria. Applicants should have completed 10+2 / PUC with Physics, Chemistry and Biology (PCB) as core subjects and obtained the minimum qualifying marks specified by S-VYASA.

02
Submit the Application Form

Eligible candidates must complete the BNYS application process by filling out the prescribed application form and submitting the required academic and personal details along with the necessary documents.

03
Appear for the Entrance Process

Admission to the BNYS programme is based on CET and the S-VYASA University Entrance Test. Candidates are required to participate in the prescribed entrance process and satisfy the admission requirements laid down by the university.

04
Verification of Academic Credentials

The university evaluates the candidate's eligibility, academic performance and supporting documents. Applicants may be required to produce original certificates and records for verification during the admission process.

05
Admission Shortlisting

Based on the candidate's performance in the admission process and fulfilment of eligibility criteria, the university prepares a list of shortlisted candidates for seat allocation.

06
Seat Confirmation

Selected candidates receive admission confirmation from the university. To secure the allotted seat, candidates must complete the prescribed admission formalities within the stipulated timeline.

07
Fee Payment and Enrollment

Candidates are required to pay the applicable tuition and admission fees to confirm their enrollment in the BNYS programme. Upon successful fee payment, admission is formally completed.

08
Reporting to Campus

After admission confirmation, students report to the Prashanti Kutiram campus to complete the final registration process and begin their academic journey in Naturopathy and Yogic Sciences.

09
Commencement of Academic Session

Students undergo orientation and induction activities before the commencement of regular classes, practical training and clinical exposure as part of the BNYS curriculum.
